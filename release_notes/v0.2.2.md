# v0.2.2 Release Notes

**Released:** 2026-06-11
**Prior version:** v0.2.1 (2026-06-09, doi:10.5281/zenodo.20615410)
**Tag:** v0.2.2
**Semver impact:** PATCH.  A bug fix and an additive read-only ledger;
`prob_floor=None` is still bit-for-bit the v0.2.0/v0.2.1 engine, and no
re-exported symbol changes.
**DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.20711380](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20711380)

## Why v0.2.2

`dcl-delta-p-min`'s Phase 2 4-cell grid exercised `prob_floor` on the
continuous tidal-ionization wavepackets (large lattices, a narrow
proton packet) for the first time and surfaced a **denormal-overflow
NaN bug** in `CausalSession._apply_prob_floor`, together with the need
to **account exactly for the probability the floor manufactures** (an
A=1 bookkeeping requirement, not just a numerical renormalisation).
v0.2.2 fixes the bug and adds the ledger.  Without it the `core`
column of the 4-cell grid (`exp_18_dp_min_grid`, `exp_09_dp_min_floor`,
`exp_12_dp_min_sweep`) produces NaN on the very first floored tick.

## Public-API changes (vs v0.2.1)

### Fixed

- **`prob_floor` denormal overflow → NaN.**  The rescale factor was
  computed as `sqrt(prob_floor / p(x))`.  A continuous wavepacket's
  exponential tail underflows `p(x)` to deep denormals
  (`p ~ 1e-323`), where the intermediate `prob_floor / p` exceeds
  `max_float` and overflows to `inf`, which then NaN-poisons the
  joint renormalisation and destroys the state on tick 0.  v0.2.2
  computes the algebraically identical `sqrt(prob_floor) / sqrt(p)`,
  keeping both operands in the normal float64 range (even the
  smallest subnormal `p ~ 1e-323` has `sqrt(p) ~ 3e-162`).  Results
  that were finite under v0.2.1 are reproduced to ~1 ulp; results
  that were NaN are now finite and A=1-consistent.

### Added

- **`CausalSession.floor_ledger()`** and the `floor_*` counters
  (`floor_manufactured_total`, `floor_n_raised_total`,
  `floor_ticks_floored`, and per-tick `_floor_*_last`).  Raising a
  sub-floor node from `p` up to `prob_floor` *manufactures*
  `(prob_floor - p)` of probability at that node; the end-of-tick
  renormalisation redistributes it so A=1 still holds, but the
  manufactured mass is a real bookkeeping quantity.  The ledger
  records it exactly.  This is the **A=1 cleanup budget**:
  `manufactured_total` is the displaced probability that conservation
  must attribute somewhere -- `dcl-delta-p-min`'s working hypothesis
  is that it is balanced by other generators in the SM zoo
  (`dcl-sm-derivation`).  All counters stay `0` when
  `prob_floor is None`.

  Quantitatively the ledger makes the cross-engine non-equivalence
  visible: a small floor (`1e-10`–`1e-6`) manufactures negligible
  mass and leaves the packet intact; an aggressive floor (`0.25`)
  manufactures mass `>> 1` and over-writes the packet toward uniform
  -- the continuous-engine analogue is *not* equivalent to `core3d`'s
  token granularity, where sub-quantum mass rounds **down** to empty
  rather than **up** to the floor.

### Changed / Removed / Deprecated

(None.  `prob_floor=None` is bit-for-bit identical to v0.2.1.)

## Tests

`pytest tests/` reports **257 passed, 1 skipped, 26 xfailed**
(v0.2.1: 252 passed).  The +5 (8 new minus reorganisation) are in
`tests/test_prob_floor_a1_consistency.py`:

- `TestProbFloorDenormalOverflow` -- a narrow Gaussian whose tail is a
  genuine deep denormal (`min p < 1e-300`) stays finite and A=1-unit
  over 20 ticks with `np.errstate(over='raise', invalid='raise')`,
  across `prob_floor in {1e-10, 1e-6, 1e-3, 0.25}`; plus a split-sqrt
  vs fused-form agreement check for normal `p`.
- `TestProbFloorLedger` -- `None` floor leaves the ledger at zero; a
  single known sub-floor node manufactures exactly `floor - p`; an
  aggressive floor manufactures `>> 1` while A=1 holds.

The 26 xfails remain pre-existing Paper~I port drift.
